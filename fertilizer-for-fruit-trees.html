Why the Right Fertilizer for Fruit Trees Is Essential

Fruit trees follow a seasonal growth cycle: initial root and shoot development, flowering and pollination, fruit set, and finally fruit growth and ripening. Each stage requires a specific nutrient profile:

  • Nitrogen (N) supports leaf and shoot growth.
  • Phosphorus (P) is vital for strong root development and flowering.
  • Potassium (K) helps with fruit formation, size, sweetness, and disease resistance.
  • Calcium and Magnesium, though secondary nutrients, play crucial roles in cell wall integrity and chlorophyll production.

A common mistake is using high-nitrogen fertilisers meant for leafy vegetables or lawns. While these might green up the canopy, they often suppress flowering and reduce fruit yields. For that reason, a specialised fertilizer for fruit trees—like the one from CompleteGrow—is key to achieving strong yields and high-quality fruit.

The Science Behind CompleteGrow’s Advanced NPK 4-18-38 + Ca & Mg

CompleteGrow’s Advanced NPK formula is not a generic blend—it’s formulated for the specific demands of fruit trees at the flowering and fruiting stages. Here’s a breakdown of why it works:

  • NPK ratio of 4-18-38: This low-nitrogen, high-phosphorus, and very high-potassium formulation is ideal for fruit trees. The nitrogen (4%) supports maintenance growth without causing overly leafy canopies. The phosphorus (18%) boosts flower and root development, while potassium (38%) ensures strong fruit set, sweetness, and resilience.
  • Calcium (Ca): Calcium is essential for strengthening cell walls in fruit, reducing issues like cracking, blossom end rot, and premature drop. It also enhances the firmness and storability of fruit.
  • Magnesium (Mg): Magnesium helps with photosynthesis, ensuring the tree can convert sunlight into energy for fruit development. It also improves nutrient transport throughout the plant.
  • Tailored for Australian conditions: Designed and manufactured in Australia, this fertiliser considers local challenges such as sandy soils, water restrictions, and harsh sun exposure—conditions that often require extra potassium and calcium for plant resilience.

This unique formulation supports not just growth, but high fruiting efficiency and quality, which is why it’s quickly gaining attention among serious backyard growers and orchardists alike.

Benefits You Can Expect for Your Fruit Trees

Enhanced Root Health: The phosphorus content supports strong, deep roots—especially valuable for young trees or those recovering from transplant shock or drought. Strong roots mean more efficient nutrient uptake and better drought resistance.

Improved Flowering and Fruit Set: During the critical blooming phase, phosphorus and potassium help drive flower production and transition into fruit. You’ll likely see better flowering intensity and a more reliable fruit set.

Bigger, Sweeter Fruit: Potassium is a key player in increasing fruit size, colour, sugar levels, and flavour. If your citrus or stone fruit often tastes bland or lacks sweetness, boosting potassium can make a noticeable difference.

Stronger, Healthier Fruit: Calcium fortifies cell walls, improving fruit firmness and shelf-life. This is particularly beneficial for commercial growers or anyone storing produce post-harvest.

Greater Resilience: The formula strengthens trees during periods of stress—whether from drought, heatwaves, or heavy fruit loads. Healthier trees are better able to defend against pests, diseases, and environmental fluctuations.

How to Apply This Fertilizer for Best Results

When to Apply: Use during early spring to support flowering and again during fruit development. Avoid applying high potassium late in the season if you want to limit late growth spurts that can reduce winter hardiness.

Application Method: This product is available in liquid form and works for both soil and foliar applications. Always mix according to label instructions to avoid overfeeding.

Irrigation Support: Ensure soil is moist before and after application. Fertiliser uptake is greatly improved when water is available, especially in warm conditions.

Frequency: Regular feeding (every 2–4 weeks during active growth) may yield better results than a single large application.

Supplementation: While this formula covers macro and key secondary nutrients, monitor for any micronutrient deficiencies, especially in older trees or those in sandy soil. A complete fertiliser program may include additional trace minerals as needed.
